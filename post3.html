Research a Typeface

July 1st, 2019

By Hai Yu Zhao

One popular font used on the web is Veranda.


The font was created by Matthew Carter and commissioned by Microsoft. It was made available in 1996.


Designed for use on-screen, the purpose of Verdana is legibility even at smaller sizes on low-resolution computer screens. This was to ensure readers distinguish between letters that are often confused at smaller sizes, such as i, j, l and the number 1.


The font resembles humanist san serifs such as Frutiger. It fairly large and wide with large x-heights, wide typeface, wide spacing and big counters. Because the letters are similar to the style students learn to print in primary grades, the font also has a simplistic feeling.


The font does not have a lot of personality but is functional and highly legible. The font’s clarity and readability are what I like most about it. On the flipside, it can be a little bit dull.


The font is commonly used in emails such as Gmail. I think this is a proper and effective use of the font because emails are primarily used to communicate information, so clarity and legibility is most important. In addition, a font with too strong of a personality may emotionally charge email messages beyond the writer’s intention.
