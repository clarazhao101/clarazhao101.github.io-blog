Wed Design Article Review

July 24th, 2019

By Hai Yu Zhao


One article on sidebar.io talks about how every product of design is one of opinion, and how if you want to produce high quality design you need develop a strong opinion, but one shaped by that of many others.


I personally partially agree and partially disagree with this thought. On one hand, I think it’s important design with purpose and that each page should have a sort of theme to it that correlates to the content. However, I think opinion is not quite the right word to use.


When I think of opinion, I think of something argumentative. I feel that it is trying to convince me to think a certain way. Even if many others think this way, I still may not feel comfortable with that.


I also think that asserting design needs an opinion, and a STRONG opinion at that, has the potential of making designers resistant to change or unopen to certain styles if said changes/styles do not fit with the “opinion” that the designer strongly holds. These changes might be the key to the business’s success.


I think effective designers should be able to adapt to ach project they are working on and not be holding on to a strong “opinion”. I don’t think we should design to try to convince others of a strongly held opinion. We can convey feelings and messages through design, but opinion just does not seem like the right word.
